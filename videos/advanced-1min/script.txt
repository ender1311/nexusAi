Nexus is a contextual multi-armed bandit. Its job is to pick the right message for every user, on every channel, and to get measurably better with each send.

It starts with the user. Ten signals — per-channel conversion rates, when someone engages, how deeply they read, whether they give — become a ten-dimensional feature vector.

Nearby vectors cluster into personas: behavioral archetypes discovered by k-means over cosine distance. Each persona gets its own learning model.

Now the choice. Every message variant is an arm. Thompson Sampling models each arm's success rate as a Beta distribution, draws a random sample from each one, and sends the highest draw. Strong arms win often, but uncertain arms still earn a shot. That's exploration and exploitation in a single rule.

When the user converts — an open, a reading plan, a donation — that becomes a reward. It's normalized, clamped, and folded back in. Good outcomes raise alpha; misses raise beta. And old evidence quietly decays, so the model stays current.

Send, observe, update — every hour, across push, email, and in-app. In production, that's a thirty-four percent lift over random. That's Nexus.
